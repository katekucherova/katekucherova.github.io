About This Project

This is an independent portfolio project, not employer work product. I built a financial analysis model using Siemens Mobility's public FY2025 results — a profit variance bridge explaining what drove the year-over-year change in EBITA, a cost-center budget-vs-actual tracker, and a monthly FY2026 revenue and margin forecast — then visualized all of it in a 3-page Power BI dashboard.

Key Insights

FY2026 forecast targets €12.0B revenue and €1.22B EBITA.

The monthly forecast model shows margin improving steadily toward the target range across the forecast window, built up from a month-by-month revenue and margin ramp rather than a flat annual assumption.

FY2025 profit improvement was driven by volume, not cost cutting.

The profit variance bridge isolates four drivers of the FY2024-to-FY2025 EBITA change: revenue volume growth contributed +€91M, the largest single driver by far, while margin/cost pressure (-€5M) and severance costs (-€7M) partially offset it. Residual/other effects added +€7M. Net: EBITA grew from €1,013M to €1,099M, and the growth was fundamentally a volume story.

Materials is the largest cost-center overrun heading into FY2026.

Of the four tracked cost centers, Materials shows the largest budget variance at +€17.02M (flagged Escalate), followed by Direct Labor at +€8.73M (Monitor). Manufacturing OH is also over budget but smaller in magnitude, while Indirect Labor is running under budget and On Track — the one favorable line among the four.

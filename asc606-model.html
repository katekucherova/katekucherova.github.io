ASC 606 Revenue Recognition Model

A cost-to-cost percentage-of-completion model built to demonstrate correct ASC 606 mechanics on a long-term contract.

This is a skill demonstration, not a case study. Unlike Claire Studio and the Siemens Mobility analysis, this model uses an illustrative contract — not real transactions or a real company. It exists to show the mechanics of ASC 606 revenue recognition are built correctly, since several roles I've applied to have asked for exactly this.
What This Demonstrates

An 18-month, $5M fixed-price equipment installation contract with four billing milestones. Revenue is recognized using the cost-to-cost method — the percentage of total estimated cost incurred each month drives how much revenue is recognized that month, independent of when the customer is actually billed.

The Five ASC 606 Steps, Applied

This is the actual ASC 606 SUMMARY tab from the workbook — not a restated summary. Click to view full size.

Built-In Self-Checks
Milestone billing percentagesMust sum to 100%
Total revenue recognizedMust equal total contract value
Ending contract asset / (liability)Must net to zero at contract close

Each month, the model also classifies the position as a contract asset (revenue recognized ahead of billing) or a contract liability (billed ahead of revenue recognized) — the core judgment ASC 606 requires.
